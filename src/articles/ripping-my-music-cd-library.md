It was 2021 and during the pandemic I started all sort of new obsessions. One of these was ripping my CD library to digital FLAC files and finally have all the music I own available for streaming in my Plex library with Plexamp. 

Why would I do such a thing when most of my music collection is easily available on Spotify or Apple Music? Well, for once, this is not 'just' a collection, but my personal collection, music I've curated since my teens, up till my thirties. There is a history here, memories, parts of my life I associate with certain soundtracks. Hell, my late teens - early twenties, was a period where I basically formed my core music taste (like most people). I can possibly tell you some trivial facts related to me or the music for about 80% of my collection. It's a huge part of me, so that's why I'm one of the few people who didn't get rid of their CD collection when I moved over to streaming.

I didn't really play these CD's for years. I didn't even own a CD player anymore since I got my old Sony back from my parent's home. I've embraced playing digital music files ever since
